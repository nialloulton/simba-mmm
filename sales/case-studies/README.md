## Representative Examples

*The following examples are based on typical results seen across Simba deployments. They illustrate common use patterns and outcomes.*

### CPG Brand — $5M Annual Media Budget Optimization

A consumer packaged goods brand with five product lines and a $5M annual media budget used Simba to replace their annual agency-led econometric study with continuous, in-house measurement.

| Metric | Before Simba | After Simba |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Measurement cadence** | Annual | Monthly |
| **Time to results** | 8–12 weeks | Same day (model refresh) |
| **Reallocation identified** | N/A | 18% of budget ($900K) moved to higher-return channels |
| **Methodology** | Black-box OLS regression | Transparent Bayesian (PyMC-Marketing) |
| **Lift test integration** | None | 3 lift tests incorporated as likelihood observations |

**Key outcome:** The brand identified that digital display was over-saturated while branded search and email had significant headroom. Reallocating 18% of the budget increased estimated total media-driven revenue by 12%.

### D2C E-Commerce — Replacing Last-Click Attribution

A direct-to-consumer e-commerce brand spending $2M/year across 6 digital channels discovered that their last-click attribution was dramatically misrepresenting channel effectiveness.

| Metric | Last-Click Attribution | Simba MMM |
|--------|----------------------|-----------|
| **TV ROAS** | Not measurable | 3.1x (94% HDI: 2.0–4.3x) |
| **Paid Social ROAS** | 4.8x (inflated by view-through) | 1.9x |
| **Branded Search ROAS** | 8.2x (capturing existing demand) | 2.4x (incremental only) |
| **Email ROAS** | 12.0x (last-touch bias) | 4.5x |

**Key outcome:** TV was 3x more effective than platform metrics could show (since TV has no click-through). The brand used Simba's Scenario Planner to model holiday budget allocation, shifting spend from over-credited channels to TV and upper-funnel social — resulting in a more balanced media mix for the peak season.
